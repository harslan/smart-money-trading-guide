üî¥ Premium Zone (Light Red/Pink Background - Top Third)

  • What it means: Price is "expensive" here
  • Analogy: You're at the Apple Store on Black Friday and iPhones are 50% above retail price
  • Trading rule: This is where you SELL or go SHORT
  • Why: Smart money is likely taking profits here; price tends to fall from expensive levels

‚ö™ Equilibrium Zone (Gray/Middle Area)

  • What it means: Fair value - price is neutral
  • Analogy: The iPhone is at regular retail price
  • Trading rule: Be cautious - wait for price to move to premium or discount

  • Analogy: The iPhone is 50% off at a clearance sale

  • Why: Smart money accumulates here; price tends to rise from cheap levels
üí° Key Insight: Always ask yourself, "Am I in premium, equilibrium, or discount?" This single question prevents 70% of bad trades.

1.2 Order Blocks (The Foundation Stones)

Order blocks appear as solid colored rectangles on your chart:

  • Dark Blue boxes = Bullish Order Blocks
  • Dark Red/Pink boxes = Bearish Order Blocks

What Are They?

Order blocks mark the last candle before a strong price move, where institutions placed massive orders.

Analogy: Imagine you're driving and see a wall. That wall didn't appear magically - someone built it (institutions). Order blocks are like seeing the foundation of that wall. When price returns to the foundation, it often "bounces" off it.

How They Form:

Bullish Order Block Example:
1. Price is falling
2. Suddenly, a strong upward move begins
3. The indicator highlights the candle BEFORE the move (where big buyers stepped in)
4. That becomes a blue support zone
Bearish Order Block Example:
1. Price is rising
2. Suddenly, a strong downward move begins
3. The indicator highlights the candle BEFORE the move (where big sellers stepped in)
4. That becomes a red resistance zone

How To Trade Them:

For LONG Trades For SHORT Trades
Wait for price to return to a bullish order block (blue box) Wait for price to return to a bearish order block (red box)
Ideally in the discount zone Ideally in the premium zone
Enter LONG when price touches the order block Enter SHORT when price touches the order block
Stop loss: Just below the order block Stop loss: Just above the order block
Target: Next resistance or opposite order block Target: Next support or opposite order block
üö® Critical Rule: Order blocks are only valid UNTIL price closes through them. Once broken, they lose their power.

1.3 Market Structure (BOS & CHoCH) - Reading the Trend

These labels appear on your chart showing institutional trend changes:

BOS (Break of Structure)

  • What it means: "Break of Structure" - the trend is CONTINUING
  • Looks like: Price breaks through a previous high (uptrend) or low (downtrend)
  • Analogy: Like a marathon runner breaking through mile markers - they're still going in the same direction, just gaining ground
In an Uptrend (Green BOS):
  • Price makes a higher high
  • Confirms buyers are in control
  • Safe to look for LONG entries
In a Downtrend (Red BOS):
  • Price makes a lower low
  • Confirms sellers are in control
  • Safe to look for SHORT entries

CHoCH (Change of Character)

  • What it means: The trend is REVERSING
  • Looks like: Price breaks structure in the OPPOSITE direction of the previous trend
  • Analogy: The marathon runner suddenly turns around and runs the other way - something changed!
Bullish CHoCH (Green):
  • Appeared during a downtrend
  • Price breaks above a recent high
  • Signals: Trend may be changing to bullish
  • Action: Stop looking for shorts; start looking for longs
Bearish CHoCH (Red):
  • Appeared during an uptrend
  • Price breaks below a recent low
  • Signals: Trend may be changing to bearish
  • Action: Stop looking for longs; start looking for shorts

How To Use BOS & CHoCH:

The Golden Rule:
  • Trade WITH BOS (following the trend)
  • Be CAUTIOUS after CHoCH (trend is changing, wait for confirmation)
Example Trading Flow:
1. See a Bullish CHoCH ‚Üí Trend may be turning up
2. Wait for a Bullish BOS ‚Üí Confirms uptrend
3. Wait for pullback to order block in discount zone
4. Enter LONG
5. Continue trading longs until you see a Bearish CHoCH

1.4 Equal Highs (EQH) and Equal Lows (EQL)

These appear as dotted lines connecting swing points at the same level, labeled "EQH" or "EQL"

What Are They?

Multiple price peaks (highs) or valleys (lows) at approximately the same price level.

Analogy: Imagine a fence with several posts at the same height. Retail traders place their stop-losses just above/below these posts. Smart money KNOWS this and hunts those stops like a predator hunting prey at a watering hole.

Why They Matter:

  • Liquidity pools: Clusters of stop-loss orders
  • Smart money intentionally breaks these levels to trigger stops
  • After the "stop hunt," price often reverses sharply

How To Trade Them:

EQH (Equal Highs) - Resistance:
  • Multiple swing highs around $470 (example)
  • Expectation: Price will likely spike ABOVE $470 briefly (stop hunt)
  • Then: Reverse downward sharply
  • Trade: Wait for price to spike above, then enter SHORT on the rejection
EQL (Equal Lows) - Support:
  • Multiple swing lows around $440 (example)
  • Expectation: Price will likely spike BELOW $440 briefly (stop hunt)
  • Then: Reverse upward sharply
  • Trade: Wait for price to spike below, then enter LONG on the bounce
‚ö†Ô∏è Warning: NEVER place your stop-loss exactly at EQH/EQL levels. Smart money will hunt them. Place stops 1-2% beyond these levels.

1.5 Fair Value Gaps (FVGs)

These are rectangular boxes showing price "gaps" - areas where price moved so fast that there was imbalanced trading.

What Are They?

When price jumps significantly in three candles, leaving a gap where minimal trading occurred.

Analogy: Imagine a highway with a missing section - eventually, engineers must return to fill that gap. The market works similarly; price tends to return to "fill" these gaps.

Types:

  • Bullish FVG (Green box): Gap created during sharp upward move
  • Bearish FVG (Red box): Gap created during sharp downward move

How To Trade Them:

When Price Approaches an FVG:

Option 1 - The Fill:
‚Ä¢ Price enters the FVG to "fill" it
‚Ä¢ Often acts as support/resistance
‚Ä¢ Enter a trade expecting price to bounce from the FVG

Option 2 - The Rejection:
‚Ä¢ Price touches the FVG edge and rejects
‚Ä¢ FVG acts as a barrier
‚Ä¢ Enter when rejection is confirmed
üéØ Pro Tip: FVGs inside order blocks are extremely powerful setups. This is confluence - multiple reasons supporting the same trade.

1.6 Strong/Weak High & Low Labels

These appear as text labels on the right side of your chart.

Label Type Meaning Trading Implication
Strong High A high made during a confirmed UPTREND (after BOS) Difficult for price to break; acts as strong resistance
Weak High A high made during uncertain or changing structure Likely to be broken soon; don't expect it to hold
Strong Low A low made during a confirmed DOWNTREND Difficult for price to break; acts as strong support
Weak Low A low made during uncertain structure Likely to be broken soon
How To Use Them:
Weak High/Low Strategy:
  • Don't assume they'll hold as support/resistance
  • Do expect them to break
  • Trade: Position for the break or take profits before them

2.4 Risk Management Rules (Non-Negotiable)

Position Sizing

  • Never risk more than 1-2% of your account on a single trade
  • Example: $10,000 account = $100-200 max risk per trade
  • If your stop loss is $5 away, you can buy: $200 risk √∑ $5 stop = 40 shares

Stop Loss Placement

  • For Longs: 1-2% below the order block
  • For Shorts: 1-2% above the order block
  • Never place stops at exact EQH/EQL levels (stop hunt zone)
  • Never widen your stop if the trade goes against you

Taking Profits

  • Scale out: Take partial profits at multiple targets
  • Don't wait for the "perfect" exit
  • Lock in profits as price moves in your favor

The 3-Strike Rule

  • If you have 3 losing trades in a row ‚Üí STOP
  • Review your trades
  • Identify what went wrong
  • Paper trade until you find your rhythm again

Part 3: Common Scenarios & How To Trade Them

Scenario 1: Price in Discount + Order Block + EQL

What you see:
  • Price has fallen to discount zone
  • There's a blue order block
  • EQL is right below the order block
What it means: Triple confluence - extremely high probability long setup

How to trade:
  1. Set a buy limit order at the order block
  2. Tight stop below the EQL (but not exactly at it)
  3. Target: Premium zone or next resistance
  4. Special note: Price may wick below EQL first (stop hunt), then bounce. Be patient.

Scenario 2: CHoCH Appears

What you see:
  • A CHoCH label appears on the chart
  • Market structure is breaking
What it means: The trend is changing

How to trade:
  1. IMMEDIATELY: Close any trades against the CHoCH direction
  2. WAIT: Don't rush into the new direction
  3. CONFIRM: Wait for a BOS in the new direction
  4. THEN: Look for pullback entries in the new trend direction

Scenario 3: Price Stuck in Equilibrium

What you see:
  • Price is in the gray middle zone
  • Bouncing around, no clear direction
What it means: No edge; market is balanced

How to trade:
  1. DO NOT TRADE
  2. Wait for price to move to premium or discount
  3. Use this time to analyze and plan
  4. Set alerts at premium and discount levels

Analogy: You're at a crossroads with no street signs. You wouldn't guess which way to go - you'd wait for a clear signal. Same here.

Scenario 4: Multiple Order Blocks Stacked

What you see:
  • Several order blocks clustered together
  • Like layers of a cake
What it means: Strong institutional zone

How to trade:
  1. Strongest setup: Enter at the FIRST order block price hits
  2. If that breaks, the next order block is your backup
  3. Place stop beyond ALL order blocks
  4. This is a "last line of defense" zone - very powerful

Scenario 5: Order Block Break

What you see:
  • Price closes through an order block you were watching
  • The order block is now "broken"
What it means: That order block is dead; no longer valid

How to trade:
  1. If you're in a trade: Exit immediately (if it's your stop level)
  2. If you're waiting to enter: Scratch that plan
  3. New opportunity: The broken order block becomes a target for the opposite direction
    • Broken bullish OB ‚Üí Now resistance for shorts
    • Broken bearish OB ‚Üí Now support for longs

Scenario 6: Fair Value Gap + Order Block Overlap

What you see:
  • An FVG box overlapping with an order block
  • Double confluence
What it means: Premium setup - institutions accumulated here twice

How to trade:
  1. This is an "A+ setup"
  2. Enter when price touches this zone
  3. Can use slightly wider stop (more room given the confidence)
  4. Often results in explosive moves

Part 5: Advanced Tips

Tip 1: Time Frames Matter

  • This indicator works on ALL timeframes
  • Higher timeframe structure always wins
  • Example: If daily chart shows bearish structure, don't force longs on 1-hour chart
  • Best practice: Check daily for overall bias, then use hourly/15-min for entries

Tip 2: Volume Confirmation

  • Strong moves breaking structure should have increased volume
  • Low volume breakouts are suspect (likely to fail)
  • Look for volume picking up with bullish/bearish moves - good sign

Tip 3: The "Weak High/Low" Secret

  • These labels are gold
  • "Weak High" means: don't expect it to hold
  • Strategy: If long, take profits BEFORE weak highs
  • If short, enter AFTER weak highs fail

Tip 4: Market Context

  • Smart Money Concepts work best in trending markets
  • In choppy, range-bound markets, reduce position size
  • During major news events, step aside (institutional behavior changes)

Tip 5: Patience is Profit

  • The indicator gives you 2-3 high-quality setups per week per stock
  • Don't force trades
  • Wait for the setup to come to you
  • Remember: Missing a trade is better than taking a bad trade
